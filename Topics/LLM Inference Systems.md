# LLM Inference Systems

## What

End-to-end systems for serving large language models — from request scheduling to kernel-level attention, with everything in between: continuous batching, paged KV management, parallelism (TP/EP/CP), comm-overlap, observability, disaggregation. The denominator is *latency × throughput Pareto* under operator SLAs (FTL, TTL). The 2025-2026 landscape has matured from "vLLM is the open-source default" to a multi-stack ecosystem (vLLM, SGLang, TensorRT-LLM, Mooncake, DeepFlow) with distinct design philosophies but converging primitives.

## Current understanding

The **Pan+Li survey** ([[2026-05-08-a-survey-of-llm-inference-systems]]) provides the unifying frame for the 2025–2026 landscape: every technique in the ecosystem maps onto three operator-spanning primitives — **load prediction** (anticipating request shape and arrival), **adaptive mechanisms** (runtime adjustment of scheduling, batching, quantization), and **cost reduction** (caching, eviction, recomputation avoidance). The survey's `cs.DB` filing is deliberate; autoregressive generation is a systems problem, and decades of database research (cost-based query optimization, MVCC, eviction policies) are under-applied to it. Named systems covered: vLLM, SGLang, Mooncake, DeepFlow — TensorRT-LLM is notably absent, likely reflecting the cs.DB framing rather than a judgment on production relevance. System composition runs single-replica → multi-replica → disaggregated → serverless, with serverless treated as the most-composed tier.

**Where the design space has settled** (2025–2026): continuous batching is the unambiguous default across [[vLLM]], [[SGLang]], and [[TensorRT-LLM]] — variation lives at the scheduler level (in-flight, piggybacked, chunked-prefill). [[PagedAttention]] is table-stakes; [[2025-12-14-geeeekexplorer-nano-vllm-nano-vllm]]'s ~1,200-line nano-vLLM reimplementation demonstrates that paged attention + prefix caching + TP + Torch compile is the **irreducible core**, reproducing ~105% of vLLM's throughput at small scale (1,434 vs 1,361 tok/s on Qwen3-0.6B, RTX 4070 Laptop). The kernel-level consequence of continuous batching is non-obvious: [[2026-01-28-flashmla-docs-20250422-new-kernel-deep-d]]'s FlashMLA deep-dive shows that MLA decoding on H800 is **compute-bound** (not memory-bound), because DeepSeek's TP=1 decoding configuration keeps `h_q = 128`, pushing the compute/memory ratio above H800's crossover (~258). The "seesaw" schedule FlashMLA uses — splitting output O_L/O_R vertically across two warpgroups — is a generalizable lesson: register-budget constraints force away from FlashAttention-3's ping-pong schedule when the output matrix (64×512 @ 32-bit = 32,768 registers) consumes half the SM's register file.

**Speculative decoding at production batch size is no longer assumed throughput-degrading.** [[2025-10-09-eagle-3-scalingupinference-acceleration-]] (EAGLE-3) shows **40% throughput improvement at batch size 64** in SGLang — previously, draft-model speculation was considered a low-batch-only optimization. The deeper finding is architectural: removing the feature-prediction loss constraint (`l_fea`) from the draft model unlocks a **scaling law for inference-acceleration training data** that didn't hold for prior EAGLE generations (up to 6.5× single-task speedup, ~4× on average at temperature=0 across 70B targets). EAGLE-3's multi-layer feature fusion and training-time test trick are principled responses to specific distribution-mismatch problems; both are downstream of the same root insight: remove the constraint, discover the scale.

**Communication-computation overlap** is a settled performance lever for TP-heavy deployments. [[2025-10-09-flux-fast-software-based-communication-o]] (Flux, ByteDance + PKU) shows that kernel-fusion comm overlap — fusing AllGather/ReduceScatter at tile granularity into a single large CUTLASS kernel — delivers **1.66× prefill / 1.30× decode** over vLLM on 8-GPU TP configurations, with up to 96% of communication hidden. Prior stream/event-based overlap (TransformerEngine) is sometimes *slower* than no-overlap at small batch sizes, because GEMM splitting underutilizes SMs as TP scales. Flux is composable with disaggregation: it improves within-pool execution, while disaggregation restructures the across-pool pipeline.

**Disaggregation is the most design-space-dependent choice.** [[2025-10-09-beyond-the-buzz-a-pragmatic-take-on-infe]] (NVIDIA, 100k+ simulated design points) is the empirical anchor: disaggregation wins are **concentrated in prefill-heavy traffic + large models (>10B)**. For small models or generation-heavy traffic, piggybacked co-located serving is competitive. The load-bearing system primitive in disaggregated deployments is **dynamic rate matching** (the Ctx:Gen GPU ratio) — a fixed ratio is Pareto-suboptimal across latency regimes (fixed 3.5 degrades as TTL tightens; fixed 0.5 degrades as TTL relaxes). The paper also closes the "KV bandwidth bottleneck" hypothesis: provisioned datacenter bandwidth is analytically sufficient for KV cache transfer across realistic SLAs. Chunked Pipeline Parallelism (CPP) for prefill — processing chunks in a pipeline, overlapping layer-N of new chunks with layer-(N+1) of old chunks — is effective for long-context prefill under tight FTL SLAs (shown on DeepSeek-R1 at ISL=256K, 64 GPUs).

**Where the design space is still splintering**:

- **Observability surface**: [[SGLang]] ([[2025-11-17-feature-sglang-tracing-fine-grained-trac]]) bets on OpenTelemetry spans with dual-visualization (Jaeger for request-centric, Perfetto for thread-centric), resolving OTel's single-context limitation for continuous batching's multi-request overlap. [[vLLM]] ([[2025-11-20-kvconnector-add-metrics-to-prometheus-gr]]) bets on Prometheus metrics via a connector-agnostic `KVConnectorStats` abstraction, making PD-disaggregated KV-transfer latency a first-class Grafana metric. Both solve the same operator need; convergence vs specialization is unresolved (tracked in [[Distributed-Serving Observability]]).
- **Hardware target**: the GPU-first assumption is almost universal (Blackwell B200/GB200/H200 across vLLM, SGLang, TRT-LLM operator recipes). [[TensorRT-LLM]]'s gpt-oss-120b deployment guide ([[2025-08-23-tensorrt-llm-docs-source-blogs-tech_blog]]) is the most prescriptive: two-mode configuration (low-latency vs max-throughput) driven by a single YAML diff (`enable_attention_dp` + MoE backend swap), with headline numbers of 420 tps/user (low-latency, 8× B200) and >20k tps/gpu (max-throughput, GB200 NVL72 system-level >1.5M tps). The CUTLASS MoE backend constraining max-throughput to pure EP is a concrete architectural trade-off.
- **TPU as a non-GPU alternative**: [[Ironwood]] ([[2026-01-12-ironwood-the-first-google-tpu-for-the-ag]], [[2026-01-09-google-tpus-explained-architecture-perfo]]) is the only inference-first chip at scale — 4,614 TFLOPS FP8, 192 GB HBM, 9,216-chip pods at 42.5 EFLOPS, GA Nov 2025. Gemini 3 is served entirely on TPU; TPU v4 delivers 1.2–1.7× A100 throughput at 53–77% of the power. Whether Ironwood's native FP8 + 192 GB HBM/chip displaces a material slice of frontier GPU inference is unresolved — the economics depend on customer access (Google Cloud only vs open market).
- **Deployment recipes**: TensorRT-LLM publishes prescriptive two-mode operator configurations; vLLM and SGLang remain framework-as-toolkit with operator-authored recipes. Whether prescriptive recipes become the industry norm is unclear.

The **cs.DB framing** from the Pan+Li survey remains the most useful cross-cutting lens: treating LLM inference as a database-systems problem — request scheduling as query planning, KV cache as buffer pool management, eviction as page replacement, disaggregation as distributed query execution — surfaces techniques from decades of prior work that haven't yet propagated through practitioner stacks. That gap is both the open research frontier and the fastest path to near-term improvements.
