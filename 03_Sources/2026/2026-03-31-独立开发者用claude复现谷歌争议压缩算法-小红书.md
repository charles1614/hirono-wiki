# [2026-03-31] 独立开发者用 Claude 复现谷歌争议压缩算法

## TL;DR

An xhs post by **智猩猩** documenting **Tom Turney** (`@no_stp_on_snek`, ex-Google) reproducing Google Research's **TurboQuant** (ICLR 2026, KV-cache compression via **PolarQuant + Walsh-Hadamard rotation**) in 7 days using Claude. Google released the paper + blog on March 24 but **did not release code** — provoking widespread skepticism about whether the headline 6.4× KV-compression / 8× attention speedup numbers were reproducible. Turney's solo reproduction (`turboquant_plus`) starts from the paper's math and **outperforms Google's published numbers** — achieving **4.6× KV cache compression** with near-zero PPL loss + **prefill speedup from 739 tok/s → 2747 tok/s (3.7×)** on a 35B MoE model running on a MacBook with 262K context. **GitHub: 2149 stars in 7 days** (Star-History chart shows steep growth). Community-verified across Apple Silicon / NVIDIA / AMD. Google still hasn't released their code at the time of this xhs post.

## Key claims

**The TurboQuant story (Google side, March 24 2026)**:

- TurboQuant paper + blog from Google Research at ICLR 2026.
- Headline claims: **PolarQuant + Walsh-Hadamard rotation** → 6.4× KV-cache compression + near-zero quality loss + 8× attention compute speedup.
- Market impact: Micron / Samsung / SK Hynix stocks dropped sharply on the news — speculation that AI memory demand would collapse if 6.4× KV compression became standard.
- **Critical caveat**: paper-only release. No code, no implementation, no verification path. Industry skepticism: "we'll believe it when we see it."

**The Tom Turney reproduction (March 24-31 2026, 7 days)**:

- **Day 1-3**: read TurboQuant math; built `turboquant_plus` Python prototype with Claude assistance.
- **Day 5-7**: speed optimization — **prefill throughput 739 tok/s → 2747 tok/s (3.7× speedup)**.
- **Result**: 35B MoE model with 262K context running on a MacBook with **4.6× KV cache compression** + near-zero PPL loss.
- **Distribution**: ported to `llama.cpp` for production-grade deployment; verified across Apple Silicon + NVIDIA + AMD by 30+ community testers.
- **GitHub**: `TheTom/turboquant_plus` — **2149 stars by 2026-03-31**, ~613 in the first week (per a verbatim quote in the captured content).

**What the reproduction proved**:

- **TurboQuant is real and reproducible** — Google's headline claims hold up when re-implemented from the paper math.
- **An individual developer with Claude can rebuild a frontier research artifact in a week**. The bottleneck was *paper-reading + mathematical-formulation translation*, not implementation effort.
- **Open-source + community-verified beats unverified-vendor-claim**, even from Google Research. Author's framing: "AI时代，大厂的论文发布后已经不再是金融业的，开发者的实际测试高于任何品牌的官方发布" — in the AI era, after a Big-Tech paper drops, the developer-community's real-world test outranks the brand's official release.

**The PolarQuant + Walsh-Hadamard combination (per the xhs body)**:

- **PolarQuant** is the new quantization scheme TurboQuant introduces.
- **Walsh-Hadamard rotation** is the math primitive that enables the compression — likely a per-block randomized linear transform that decorrelates KV entries before quantization, similar in spirit to QuIP# / [[2026-02-04-pretraining-large-language-models-with-n]]'s Random Hadamard Transforms in NVFP4 training.

**Cross-reference to NVFP4 training-side use of RHT**: the NVFP4 paper ([[2026-02-04-pretraining-large-language-models-with-n]]) lists **Random Hadamard Transforms applied per-block to bound outliers** as ingredient #1 of its 4-ingredient FP4-training recipe. TurboQuant applies the same Hadamard-rotation idea at *inference time* for KV-cache compression. **Both training-quantization (NVFP4) and inference-KV-quantization (TurboQuant) converge on Hadamard-rotation as the load-bearing outlier-control primitive.**
