# [2025-11-02] 别被100%骗了：GPU利用率背后的真相

## TL;DR

Translation and commentary on a Trainy.ai blog post revealing that GPU utilization (nvidia-smi) reports whether any kernel is running — not how much compute is being used — explaining why a training run can show 100% utilization while achieving only 20% MFU, and how replacing unfused kernels with fused implementations raised MFU from 20% to 38% and cut training time by 4×.

## Key claims

- GPU utilization = "percentage of time one or more kernels were executing on the GPU" (Datadog NVML docs); a pure memory-copy operation with zero FLOPs achieves 100% utilization.
- MFU (Model FLOPS Utilization), introduced by Google's PaLM paper, is observed throughput (tokens/sec) ÷ theoretical peak throughput; typical LLM training targets 35–45% MFU.
- A production training run at 100% GPU utilization measured only ~20% MFU, a ~5× gap attributable to memory-bound unfused kernels.
- [[SM Efficiency]] (SM Activity) = fraction of active SMs over a time window; a kernel using 1 of 132 SMs on H100 for 10 seconds shows 100% utilization but 0.7% SM efficiency.
- Root cause: Softmax kernel (memory-bound) showed high utilization but low SM efficiency in PyTorch Profiler; FlashAttention-style kernel fusion eliminates the bottleneck.
- Fused kernels (MLP, dropout, LayerNorm, residual add, attention) from libraries like FlashAttention provided as `nn.Module` drop-ins — no custom CUDA required; `torch.compile` was insufficient due to FSDP incompatibility and graph breaks.
- Final result: 4× training speedup, MFU from 20% → 38%, by fusing key Transformer operations and selecting correct model parallelism for 3.2 Tbps InfiniBand.
- NVIDIA DCGM includes SM Activity metric by default; SM Occupancy (Achieved Occupancy in PyTorch Profiler) is finer-grained but less actionable.
