# [2017-08-18] 答题 | 高速信号编码之8B/10B

## TL;DR

Community Q&A follow-up to a primer on [[8B10B Encoding]], collecting reader answers on why encoding is used in high-speed serial links. Multiple respondents with scores 2–3 explain DC balance, eye diagram improvement, clock recovery, and inter-symbol interference (ISI) reduction.

## Key claims

- Root purpose of [[8B10B Encoding]]: DC balance — encoding converts low-frequency patterns into high-frequency equivalents, passing AC-coupling capacitors with lower loss (capacitor impedance Zc=1/2πfC is high at low frequency).
- Frequency spectrum comparison of prbs7 at 10 Gbps: with 8B/10B encoding, low-frequency energy is clearly reduced and redistributed to higher frequencies — equivalent to a high-pass filter.
- Clock recovery benefit: reduced consecutive 0s/1s gives CDR PLL more edge transitions, enabling accurate embedded-clock extraction and reduced jitter.
- Additional benefits: error detection via special code patterns, data alignment control codes, embedded encryption effect.
- 8B/10B achieves at most 5 consecutive identical bits; overhead is 20%; higher-efficiency alternatives (64B/66B, 128B/130B) exist for higher-rate protocols.
- PCIe Gen1 runs at 2.5 Gbps with 8B/10B giving 2 Gbps effective; PCIe Gen2 at 5 Gbps giving 4 Gbps effective; later generations switched to 128B/130B.
