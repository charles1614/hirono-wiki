# [2025-06-11] 异步RL框架AReaL速览

## TL;DR

Technical overview of [[AReaL]] (inclusionAI's async RL framework), explaining how it eliminates GPU bubbles in synchronous RL and enables rollout-trainer overlap by allowing a single sequence's response to be generated across multiple weight checkpoints — and introducing "decouple PPO" to maintain a stable trust region in this asynchronous setting.

## Key claims

- Synchronous RL has two inefficiencies: (1) within the rollout stage, short sequences wait for long sequences to finish before releasing results; (2) rollout and trainer are serialized — when one runs, the other idles.
- AReaL's solution: when the trainer finishes a weight update (θ_t → θ_{t+1}) and broadcasts it, rollout immediately interrupts the in-progress sequence, saves the partial response, clears associated KV cache, then resumes from prefill with the new weights — the final sequence may be `(prompt, response₀, response₁, …, responseₙ)` where each segment was generated by a different θ.
- This allows rollout to receive a larger-than-training-batch prompt stream and emit completed samples as they finish, so the trainer always has data ready (eliminating the rollout internal bubble and enabling overlap).
- **Decouple [[PPO]]**: in standard PPO, the old policy logP and the current policy logP bound the trust region; in AReaL the "old policy" is a mixture of multiple θ values, making the trust region unreliable. The fix introduces a separate reference policy π_behave (the most recent θ that generated each segment) to construct a tighter trust region, while proving algebraically that the gradient direction and magnitude when unclipped remain identical to standard PPO.
- Implementation detail: behave-related logP is computed by the rollout backend ([[SGLang]]/vLLM); the other two logP terms are computed by the trainer backend ([[DeepSpeed]]/FSDP/Megatron); framework precision differences cause numerical mismatches that remain an open engineering issue.
