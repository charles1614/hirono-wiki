# [2026-02-08] Brendan Gregg 从 Intel 到 OpenAI 的性能工程新征程

## TL;DR
Brendan Gregg left Intel after 3.5 years (including 15 months of hiring freezes) and joined OpenAI in early 2026 as a Member of Technical Staff on the ChatGPT performance engineering team, reporting to Justin Becker, working remotely from Sydney. His core thesis: AI data center cost growth is outpacing traditional performance engineering methods, and the field needs new approaches at every layer.

## Key claims
- At Intel, Gregg open-sourced AI Flame Graphs, advanced Linux stack-walking, led the eBPF technical steering committee, and authored a 33-recommendation cloud strategy spanning 19 teams.
- After leaving Intel, Gregg underwent 26 interviews before choosing [[OpenAI]]; he explicitly compared working at OpenAI to his Netflix years — same hyperscale cloud challenges, fast iteration, and room for real impact.
- His stated motivation: AI data center costs are "rising at an alarming rate" and "saving costs is saving the planet"; existing performance engineering methods may no longer suffice at this scale.
- His first project at OpenAI is a cross-organizational performance optimization strategy; planned tools include eBPF, Ftrace, and PMCs adapted to AI data center use.
- He plans to apply his classic techniques — eBPF, Ftrace, PMCs — to AI data center contexts, and stated that OpenAI is actively hiring performance engineers.
- A conversation with a hairdresser who used ChatGPT "twenty-four seven" crystallized his sense that the product had crossed into everyday human use — a key personal motivator alongside childhood AI aspirations.
