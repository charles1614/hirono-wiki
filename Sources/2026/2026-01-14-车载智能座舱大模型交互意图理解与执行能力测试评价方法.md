# [2025-08-20] 车载智能座舱大模型交互意图理解与执行能力测试评价方法 (T/CSAE Draft Standard)

## TL;DR

Draft Chinese group standard (T/CSAE xxx-2025) from the China Society of Automotive Engineers defining an evaluation framework for LLMs in smart vehicle cockpits, covering 3 top-level dimensions and 10 sub-indicators for intent understanding, execution quality, and execution efficiency, plus detailed scoring rubrics and test procedures.

## Key claims

- Three primary evaluation dimensions: 意图理解能力 (intent understanding capability), 执行质量 (execution quality), and 执行效率 (execution efficiency), with 10 second-level indicators in total.
- Intent understanding sub-indicators: 直接指令识别能力 (direct command recognition), 复杂指令识别能力 (complex command recognition — multi-task in single utterance), 模糊意图识别能力 (fuzzy intent recognition — emotion/preference/scene), 上下文理解能力 (context understanding in multi-turn dialogue), and 拒识准确率 (rejection accuracy for out-of-scope or unsafe commands).
- Execution quality sub-indicators: 任务完成率 (task completion rate), 跨域协作能力 (cross-domain collaboration across navigation/entertainment/vehicle control/communication), 文本生成质量 (text generation quality — safety/reliability/accuracy/fluency), and 图像生成质量 (image generation quality).
- Execution efficiency sub-indicators: 首字响应延迟 (TTFT — time from last word of voice command to first character presented), 文本生成速率 (characters/second), and 图像生成速率 (seconds per image).
- Scoring uses a 5-point Likert scale with explicit rubrics per indicator; e.g., direct command recognition score of 5 requires both correct understanding and successful execution; score of 2 means partial understanding but failed execution (system says unsupported or asks user to operate manually).
- Safety hard requirement: in-vehicle LLMs must never cross legal, ethical, safety, or privacy boundaries in any interaction; examples include generating illegal/violent/discriminatory content, exposing private data, or providing advice that endangers driving safety.
- Standard applies to product definition, development, acceptance, and testing phases; drafted by Tongji University plus 19 organizations including Zeekr, SAIC Volkswagen, iFlytek, Li Auto, Xiaomi Auto, Dongfeng, FAW, BMW China, and Bosch.
