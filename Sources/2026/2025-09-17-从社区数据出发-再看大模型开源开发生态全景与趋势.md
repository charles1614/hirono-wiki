# [2025-09-13] 从社区数据出发，再看大模型开源开发生态全景与趋势

## TL;DR

Ant Group Open Source's v2.0 landscape report on the LLM open source ecosystem, using OpenRank (GitHub collaboration-graph algorithm) to rank 114 projects across 22 domains, with 100-day delta analysis versus the v1.0 report, and country-level developer contribution breakdowns.

## Key claims

- The v2.0 landscape covers 114 projects (threshold: OpenRank ≥ 50 as of Aug 2025) across 22 domains; 39 new projects entered (35% of total), 60 projects dropped from v1.0 — many exiting toward the "AI graveyard."
- OpenRank top-10 most active projects cover the full LLM stack: PyTorch, Ray (infra/training); Airflow (data pipeline); vLLM, SGLang, TensorRT-LLM (serving); Dify, n8n (agent platform); Gemini CLI, Cherry Studio (end-user tools) — Python dominates infra, TypeScript dominates application layer.
- Developer landscape: 366,521 total contributors across 170+ projects; US leads at 37.4% of contribution, China at 18.7%, Germany 3rd at 6.5%; in AI Infra, US+China >60%; in AI Agent, China (24.6%) nearly matches US (21.5%).
- AI Coding, Model Serving, and LLMOps show growth trajectories in 2025; Agent Framework and AI Data show decline — LangChain, LlamaIndex, AutoGen community investment reductions are cited as a driver for Agent Framework decline.
- License fragmentation is accelerating: Dify uses modified Apache 2.0 (restricts multi-tenant SaaS); n8n uses fair-code Sustainable Use License; Cherry Studio uses dual-license (AGPLv3 for ≤10 person orgs, commercial license otherwise); even closed-source products (Cursor, Claude Code) use GitHub for community presence with many Stars.
- Project age: median age is 30 months (2.5 years); 62% launched after the "GPT moment" (Oct 2022); 12 new in 2025; average star count ~30,000.

## What this changes
