# H800

NVIDIA Hopper GPU; export-controlled variant of H100 (cut interconnect bandwidth); widely used in China.

## Synthesis


The H800 is NVIDIA's export-controlled variant of the H100 designed for the Chinese data-center market; its principal hardware difference from the H100 is reduced inter-chip bandwidth, while its intra-chip characteristics — 3.35 TB/s HBM bandwidth and a throttled ~865 TFlops practical peak (clocked to ~1600 MHz from a 990 TFlops ceiling) — are close enough to the H100 SXM5 that it has become the canonical reference platform for Chinese AI teams. DeepSeek's FlashMLA new decoding kernel is tuned specifically for H800 SXM5: by avoiding tensor parallelism on decoding instances, DeepSeek keeps h_q at 128, which pushes the compute-to-memory ratio above the H800's crossover threshold of ~258 and makes MLA decoding compute-bound rather than memory-bound — the kernel exploits this to reach ~80% Tensor Core utilization and ~3 TB/s memory throughput. ByteDance's Flux communication-overlap system treats the H800 as one of its two primary evaluation targets alongside the A100, auto-tuning its CUTLASS-based kernel-fusion implementation across both GPU generations and across PCIe and NVLink interconnects, achieving up to 1.24× training throughput improvement over Megatron-LM on 128-GPU A100/H800 clusters.
