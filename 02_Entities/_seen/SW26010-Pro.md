# SW26010-Pro

Chinese HPC processor by Sunway; successor to SW26010; features 384 CPEs per chip with DDR4 memory

## Observations

- SW26010-Pro (2020–21 era) has 6 Core Groups of 64 CPEs each (384 CPEs/chip), 512-bit vector units at 2.25 GHz, 96 GB DDR4 with 307.2 GB/s aggregate bandwidth, and only a 256 KB software-managed scratchpad per cluster (no large L2 cache); arithmetic intensity of 0.11 bytes/FP32-FLOP is ~half a consumer GPU, making the chip severely memory-bound despite peak throughput exceeding Fujitsu's A64FX. — [[2025-07-04-china-s-new-ish-sw26010-pro-supercompute]]
- The new Sunway supercomputer uses 107,136 SW26010-Pro chips (41.1M CPEs) in a three-level fat-tree topology; per-node bandwidth to the global interconnect is ~10.54 GB/s vs. Fugaku's 34 GB/s, limiting scalability for large distributed workloads; achieving good HPL-MxP performance required a research paper-level manual optimization (Rongfen Lin et al.) that would have been unnecessary on Frontier or Fugaku. — [[2025-07-04-china-s-new-ish-sw26010-pro-supercompute]]
