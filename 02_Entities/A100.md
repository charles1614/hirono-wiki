# A100

NVIDIA Ampere-architecture datacenter GPU (compute capability 8.0); production workhorse before Hopper.

## Synthesis




NVIDIA's A100 is the Ampere-architecture datacenter GPU (released 2020) that serves as the primary cross-generation baseline in modern microbenchmark and kernel-fusion studies. In HKUST's Hopper microbenchmark, A100 is the Ampere anchor whose Tensor Core precision support — INT8, INT4, FP64, BF16, TF32, sparsity — expanded over Volta's FP16/FP32 and predates Hopper's qualitative FP8 jump for LLMs. ByteDance's Flux paper treats A100 as one of two primary evaluation targets alongside H800, measuring CUTLASS-based modular tile-tuning across PCIe and NVLink intra-node interconnects and reporting up to 1.24× training throughput over Megatron-LM on 128 A100/H800 GPUs — the auto-tuner's optimal tile-size and execution-order choices depending on the specific interconnect. Google's TPU v4 positioning (Jouppi et al., 2023) used equivalent A100 pods as the comparison point claiming 1.2–1.7× higher throughput at 53–77% of the power, though the IntuitionLabs report flags these numbers may not hold against H100 or B200. The inference.ai GPU calculator lists A100 SXM at 80 GB VRAM (same tier as H100), and for FP32+Adam training of a 3.77B-parameter model requiring 62 GB, A100 SXM is the minimum data-center GPU capable (78% utilization).
