# A100

NVIDIA Ampere-architecture datacenter GPU (compute capability 8.0); production workhorse before Hopper.

## Synthesis

NVIDIA's 2020 Ampere-architecture datacenter GPU — the workhorse generation that established broad Tensor Core precision support (INT8/INT4/FP64/BF16/TF32 plus sparsity, going beyond Volta's FP16/FP32). The competitive comparison baseline in Google's TPU positioning (TPU v4 claimed 1.2-1.7× higher throughput at 53-77% power) and the Ampere cross-reference in HKUST's Hopper microbenchmark study. Flux's communication-overlap evaluation auto-tunes per-arch via CUTLASS across A100 + H800 / PCIe + NVLink combinations.

## Observations

- One of Flux's evaluation targets — auto-tunable kernel-fusion comm overlap measured across A100 + H800 GPUs and PCIe + NVLink intra-node interconnects, via CUTLASS-based modular tile-tuning. — [[2025-10-09-flux-fast-software-based-communication-o]]
- The competitive comparison point in Google's TPU positioning — TPU v4 pods claimed to deliver 1.2-1.7× higher throughput than equivalent A100 pods at 53-77% of the power, with ~3× less electricity and ~20× lower CO₂ vs on-prem GPU servers. Numbers from 2023 Jouppi et al.; the IntuitionLabs report flags they may not hold against H100 / B200. — [[2026-01-09-google-tpus-explained-architecture-perfo]]
- The Ampere cross-comparison anchor in HKUST's Hopper microbenchmark study — Tensor Core precision support expanded here over Volta's FP16/FP32: INT8, INT4, FP64, BF16, TF32, sparsity. — [[2026-01-15-benchmarking-and-dissecting-the-nvidia-h]]
