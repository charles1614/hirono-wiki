# B200

NVIDIA Blackwell datacenter GPU, second-generation tensor-core silicon at the high end.

## Synthesis



B200 is NVIDIA's Blackwell-architecture datacenter GPU (single Blackwell die in HGX form-factor, paired CPUs in GB200 form), with the JAX Scaling Book reporting 148 SMs, 192 GB HBM at 8 TB/s (versus H100's 80 GB at 3.35 TB/s), 2.25 PFLOPs BF16, and 4.5 PFLOPs FP8 — Tensor Core size grew to ~2048 FLOPs/cycle (versus H100's ~1024), and the chip adds 256 kB TMEM per SM because the Blackwell TC accumulator no longer fits in SMEM/registers. NVLink 5.0 provides 18 links × 50 GB/s = 900 GB/s per GPU, and critical arithmetic intensity is ≈ 281. B200 is the primary benchmark target for FP4 MoE inference research: on GPT-OSS-20B (32 experts, top-4, NVFP4), SGLang achieves 1168 TFLOPS versus vLLM's 1026 TFLOPS — a 142 TFLOPS gap driven by the Blackwell-native CUTLASS schedule `KernelPtrArrayTmaWarpSpecialized1SmNvf4Sm100` requiring 128-byte TMA alignment enforced via padding, with generic CUTLASS 3.x without this padding risking TMA stalls. With 142 SMs (per benchmark) and 128-token tiling, batch size 1 launches only 2 thread blocks leaving 98.6% of SMs idle without adaptive grid sizing — explaining why kernel engineering rather than hardware availability drives the SGLang-vs-vLLM gap on Blackwell.
