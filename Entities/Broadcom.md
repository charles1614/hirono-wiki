## Observations

- Co-designer of [[Google]]'s [[TPU]] silicon; earns high margin on the chip-level BOM (the largest system component). Even with Broadcom's margin stack, Google's all-in Ironwood TCO is ~44% lower than a GB200 server. [[Anthropic]] is cited as the fourth external TPU customer referenced in Broadcom's most recent earnings call, with 400k [[Ironwood]] TPUv7 units sold directly by Broadcom to Anthropic. — [[2026-01-22-google-tpuv7-the-900lb-gorilla-in-the-ro]]
- Co-developing [[OpenAI]]'s first custom AI inference processor (announced October 2025): design phase complete by early 2026; first deployment targeted H2 2026 for "o1"-series inference. Ethernet-based interconnect fabric. 10 GW target by 2029 at ~$50–60B/GW. Broadcom has since agreed to similar custom chip deals with Google and Anthropic, confirming it as the primary ASIC partner across frontier AI labs pursuing custom silicon diversification away from NVIDIA. — [[2026-01-22-oracle-openai-s-300b-deal-ai-infrastruct]]
- Codename "Titan": [[OpenAI]]'s custom chip co-developed with Broadcom on TSMC 3nm; team led by Richard Ho (~40 engineers); mass production targeted H2 2026. Ethernet-based interconnect. [[Stargate]] plans to deploy Titan across 10 GW capacity by 2029, positioning Broadcom as the silicon backbone of OpenAI's long-term NVIDIA independence strategy. — [[2026-01-22-openai-s-stargate-project-a-guide-to-the]]
- [[OpenAI]] 与 Broadcom 单独签署 10 GW 算力部署协议（2025 年 10 月），Broadcom 股价涨近 10%。 — [[2026-01-15-economictimes-indiatimes-com]]
- ByteDance's veRoCE RDMA protocol lists Broadcom as one of five NIC hardware partners for verification and deployment support; Broadcom NICs covering 400G, 800G, and 1.6T are in progressive veRoCE support rollout. — [[2025-12-19-火山引擎-force-大会发布-veroce-传输协议]]
