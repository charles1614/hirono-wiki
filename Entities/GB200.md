# GB200

NVIDIA Grace-Blackwell superchip (Grace CPU + 2× B200); deployed in GB200 NVL72 racks (72 chips).

## Synthesis



GB200 is NVIDIA's Grace-Blackwell Superchip — a Grace CPU coherently paired with two Blackwell GPUs via NVLink-C2C — packaged into NVL72 racks with 18 Compute Trays (36 Grace CPUs, 72 Blackwell GPUs) plus 9 NVSwitch Trays delivering 13.8 TB HBM3e + 17 TB LPDDR5X ≈ 30 TB fast memory at 120 kW per cabinet (versus GH200 NVL32's 40 kW). The GB200 SuperPod composes 8 NVL72 = 576 GPUs requiring 144 L1 + 72 L2 NVSwitch Trays for a 1 PB/s NVLink fabric, and GTC headline numbers report 3× training improvement on GPT-MoE-1.8T and 15× inference (3.5→58 tok/s) versus HGX H100. vLLM Wide-EP on GB200 achieves 26.2K prefill TPGS and 10.1K decode TPGS for DeepSeek MoE (2K in + 2K out) at 3–5× over H200, using 4 prefill instances (2 GPUs each) + 1 decode instance (8 GPUs) with NVFP4 GEMM and dispatch, kernel fusion, and weight-offloading-v2 async prefetch via NVLink-C2C. vLLM's gpt-oss-120b optimization on GB200 achieves 38% higher max throughput and 13% lower min latency versus the InferenceMAX baseline, with async scheduling yielding ~10% improvement and Stream Interval at 1024 concurrent requests delivering 57% end-to-end improvement by reducing host-CPU I/O overhead — the bottleneck on GB200 being CPU-side rather than GPU-side.
